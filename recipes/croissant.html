Croissant

Description

Croissants are iconic French pastries known for their crescent shape, buttery flavor, and flaky texture. Made from laminated dough, they have multiple layers that create a light, airy interior and a crisp, golden-brown exterior. Perfectly paired with a hot cup of coffee, croissants are a breakfast staple and a popular choice for any time of the day.

Ingredients

  • cooking spray
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 8 large croissants (about 1 1/4 pounds), halved horizontally
  • confectioner's sugar, fresh berries, or maple syrup for serving

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Generously coat a 9x13-inch baking dish with nonstick cooking spray.
  2. Gather all ingredients. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Generously coat a 9x13-inch baking dish with nonstick cooking spray.
  3. Dip each croissant half in the egg mixture until fully soaked.
  4. Arrange dipped croissants in the prepared baking dish, overlapping as needed. Pour any remaining egg mixture over croissants.
  5. Arrange dipped croissants in the prepared baking dish, overlapping as needed. Pour any remaining egg mixture over croissants.
  6. Let stand 15 minutes before serving. Serve with desired toppings.
